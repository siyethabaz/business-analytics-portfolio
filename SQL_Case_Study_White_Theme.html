Business Case
Reducing Repeat Contacts & SLA Breaches at a BPO Contact Centre
A Senior BA is engaged to analyse contact centre operational data and surface the drivers of repeat contacts, SLA breaches, and agent performance gaps using SQL.

🔴 The Problem

The contact centre is experiencing a 28% repeat contact rate — customers calling back within 7 days on the same issue. SLA breach costs are rising and leadership lacks visibility into which contact types, agents, and time periods are driving the problem.

🔵 The Action

The BA extracts and analyses data from the contact centre CRM database using SQL — identifying contact drivers, agent performance, repeat contact patterns, AHT trends, and monetary breach costs by team and segment.

🟢 The Result

3 contact reason codes account for 67% of all repeat contacts. Top 2 underperforming agents contribute 34% of their team's SLA breach cost. Targeted L&D and process fixes are recommended with a projected 18% reduction in repeat contacts.

Queries
Q1 Contact Volume & SLA Breach Summary by Month
Intermediate

Business Question

Leadership needs a monthly overview of contact volume, resolution rates, SLA compliance, and total breach cost to identify which months require deeper investigation.

Concepts Used

GROUP BY FORMAT() COUNT / SUM / AVG ROUND / CAST ORDER BY 
-- Monthly contact performance summary
-- Answers: where are our SLA and resolution gaps?

SELECT
    FORMAT(contact_date, 'yyyy-MM')        AS month,
    COUNT(contact_id)                      AS total_contacts,
    SUM(resolved_flag)                     AS resolved,
    SUM(sla_breach_flag)                   AS sla_breaches,
    ROUND(
        CAST(SUM(resolved_flag) AS FLOAT)
        / COUNT(contact_id) * 100, 1
    )                                       AS resolution_rate_pct,
    ROUND(
        CAST(SUM(sla_breach_flag) AS FLOAT)
        / COUNT(contact_id) * 100, 1
    )                                       AS breach_rate_pct,
    ROUND(AVG(handle_time_sec) / 60.0, 1) AS avg_aht_mins,
    ROUND(SUM(sla_breach_cost), 2)         AS total_breach_cost_r

FROM  contacts
WHERE contact_date >= '2025-01-01'
GROUP BY FORMAT(contact_date, 'yyyy-MM')
ORDER BY month;

Q2 Top Contact Drivers by Repeat Contact Rate
Intermediate

Business Question

Which contact reason codes generate the most repeat contacts within 7 days? These are "failure demand" contacts — calls that shouldn't happen if the first contact resolved the issue.

Concepts Used

Self JOIN DATEDIFF() BETWEEN GROUP BY HAVING ORDER BY DESC 
-- Identify failure demand by contact reason
-- Repeat = same customer calls again within 7 days

SELECT
    c1.contact_reason,
    COUNT(c1.contact_id)                      AS total_contacts,
    COUNT(c2.contact_id)                      AS repeat_contacts,
    ROUND(
        CAST(COUNT(c2.contact_id) AS FLOAT)
        / COUNT(c1.contact_id) * 100, 1
    )                                          AS repeat_rate_pct,
    ROUND(AVG(c1.handle_time_sec) / 60.0, 1) AS avg_aht_mins

FROM  contacts c1
LEFT JOIN contacts c2
    ON  c1.customer_id  = c2.customer_id
    AND c1.contact_id   != c2.contact_id
    AND DATEDIFF(day, c1.contact_date,
                      c2.contact_date)
        BETWEEN 1 AND 7

GROUP BY c1.contact_reason
HAVING  COUNT(c1.contact_id) > 50
ORDER BY repeat_rate_pct DESC;

BA Insight: The self-JOIN pattern links a customer's first contact to any repeat within 7 days. Payment Query at 44.9% repeat rate signals a resolution quality problem, not a volume problem. This is the anchor finding that shapes the entire business case.

Q3 Agent Performance Scorecard — AHT, FCR & SLA
Intermediate

Business Question

Which agents are underperforming on FCR and SLA compliance? Rank agents within their team and flag outliers for coaching or L&D escalation.

Concepts Used

JOIN (multi-table) GROUP BY CASE WHEN DATEADD / GETDATE HAVING ORDER BY multi-col 
-- Agent performance scorecard with action flag
-- CASE WHEN converts data into L&D action categories

SELECT
    a.team_name,
    a.agent_name,
    a.tenure_months,
    COUNT(c.contact_id)                       AS total_handled,
    ROUND(AVG(c.handle_time_sec) / 60.0, 1)  AS avg_aht_mins,
    ROUND(
        SUM(c.resolved_flag) * 100.0
        / COUNT(c.contact_id), 1
    )                                           AS fcr_pct,
    SUM(c.sla_breach_flag)                    AS sla_breaches,
    ROUND(SUM(c.sla_breach_cost), 2)          AS breach_cost_r,
    CASE
        WHEN SUM(c.resolved_flag) * 100.0
             / COUNT(c.contact_id) >= 85
        THEN 'On Target'
        WHEN SUM(c.resolved_flag) * 100.0
             / COUNT(c.contact_id) >= 75
        THEN 'Needs Coaching'
        ELSE 'Escalate to L&D'
    END                                         AS performance_flag

FROM  contacts c
JOIN  agents   a ON c.agent_id = a.agent_id
WHERE a.status = 'Active'
  AND c.contact_date >=
      DATEADD(month, -3, GETDATE())
GROUP BY
    a.team_name, a.agent_name, a.tenure_months
HAVING  COUNT(c.contact_id) > 100
ORDER BY a.team_name, fcr_pct ASC;

Q4 Hourly Contact Volume Heatmap (Peak Period Analysis)
Advanced

Business Question

At what hours and days do contact volumes peak? This informs staffing models and shift planning to reduce queue breaches caused by under-resourcing during high-demand windows.

Concepts Used

DATEPART() PIVOT via CASE WHEN GROUP BY multi-col SUM / COUNT ORDER BY 
-- Hourly volume pivoted by day of week
-- Informs workforce management and shift planning

SELECT
    DATEPART(hour, contact_date)   AS hour_of_day,
    SUM(CASE WHEN
        DATEPART(weekday, contact_date)
        = 2 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)          AS monday,
    SUM(CASE WHEN
        DATEPART(weekday, contact_date)
        = 3 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)          AS tuesday,
    SUM(CASE WHEN
        DATEPART(weekday, contact_date)
        = 4 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)          AS wednesday,
    SUM(CASE WHEN
        DATEPART(weekday, contact_date)
        = 5 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)          AS thursday,
    SUM(CASE WHEN
        DATEPART(weekday, contact_date)
        = 6 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)          AS friday

FROM  contacts
WHERE contact_date >=
      DATEADD(month, -1, GETDATE())
GROUP BY DATEPART(hour, contact_date)
ORDER BY hour_of_day;

Q5 Month-on-Month SLA Breach Cost Trend (Window Function)
Expert

Business Question

How is total SLA breach cost trending month-on-month per team? Leadership needs the running total AND the month-on-month change to evaluate whether interventions are working.

Concepts Used

CTE LAG() SUM() OVER PARTITION BY ROWS BETWEEN Window Functions 
-- MoM breach cost with running total per team
-- LAG() compares this month to last month

WITH monthly_breach AS (
    SELECT
        a.team_name,
        FORMAT(c.contact_date,
               'yyyy-MM')              AS month,
        ROUND(SUM(c.sla_breach_cost),
              2)                         AS breach_cost,
        SUM(c.sla_breach_flag)          AS breach_count
    FROM  contacts c
    JOIN  agents   a
          ON c.agent_id = a.agent_id
    GROUP BY
        a.team_name,
        FORMAT(c.contact_date, 'yyyy-MM')
)
SELECT
    team_name,
    month,
    breach_cost,
    breach_count,
    LAG(breach_cost) OVER (
        PARTITION BY team_name
        ORDER BY month
    )                               AS prev_month_cost,
    ROUND(breach_cost -
        LAG(breach_cost) OVER (
            PARTITION BY team_name
            ORDER BY month), 2)    AS mom_change_r,
    ROUND(SUM(breach_cost) OVER (
        PARTITION BY team_name
        ORDER BY month
        ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED
        PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW),
        2)                          AS running_total_r

FROM  monthly_breach
ORDER BY team_name, month;

Q6 Customer Segment × Contact Reason Matrix (Failure Demand Segmentation)
Expert

Business Question

Which customer segments generate the most repeat contacts per reason code? This enables targeted fixes — if "Payment Query" repeats are concentrated in SME, the fix is segment-specific, not a blanket change.

Concepts Used

Chained CTEs WHERE EXISTS RANK() OVER PARTITION BY 3-table JOIN Correlated Subquery 
-- Failure demand by customer segment + reason
-- WHERE EXISTS isolates repeat contacts efficiently

WITH repeat_contacts AS (
    SELECT
        c1.contact_id,
        c1.customer_id,
        c1.contact_reason,
        c1.contact_date
    FROM  contacts c1
    WHERE EXISTS (
        SELECT 1
        FROM   contacts c2
        WHERE  c2.customer_id = c1.customer_id
          AND  c2.contact_id  != c1.contact_id
          AND  DATEDIFF(day,
                   c1.contact_date,
                   c2.contact_date)
               BETWEEN 1 AND 7
    )
),
segment_matrix AS (
    SELECT
        cu.segment,
        rc.contact_reason,
        COUNT(rc.contact_id)     AS repeat_count,
        RANK() OVER (
            PARTITION BY cu.segment
            ORDER BY
            COUNT(rc.contact_id) DESC
        )                        AS rank_in_segment
    FROM  repeat_contacts rc
    JOIN  customers       cu
          ON rc.customer_id = cu.customer_id
    GROUP BY cu.segment, rc.contact_reason
)
SELECT
    segment,
    contact_reason,
    repeat_count,
    rank_in_segment
FROM  segment_matrix
WHERE rank_in_segment <= 3
ORDER BY segment, rank_in_segment;
