SLA Breach Quantification & Operational Issues Register
Independently designed and delivered
The Problem
No centralised visibility of operational failures, their root causes, or their financial cost. Leadership could not differentiate between systemic issues and isolated incidents.
The Action
Designed an Issues Register from scratch — capturing system, process, and training failures. Built an LPI dashboard quantifying the monetary cost of poor execution per incident, bucketed by impact area and proposed remedy.
The Result
R2,097 in SLA breach costs identified and visualised. 58% of failures traced to people/training gaps — enabling targeted L&D intervention rather than blanket process changes.

Trend Analysis / Root Cause
New Business Submissions Trend Analysis
Independently designed and delivered
The Problem
Declining new business submission quality with no executive visibility on where, why, or when performance was deteriorating across 3 quarters of data.
The Action
Conducted a full root cause analysis across Q2–Q4, bucketing findings into defined failure categories. Built a Power BI dashboard translating raw operational data into quarter-on-quarter trend insights adopted by leadership.
The Result
Q3 late submission rate spike (11% to 39%) identified and attributed. Q4 SLA compliance recovered to 96% — validating corrective action. Average processing time reduced from 56.7 to 49.8 days.

SQL Analytics
Contact Centre Failure Demand & Performance Analytics
Portfolio case study — original construct
The Problem
A contact centre experiencing a 28% repeat contact rate with no structured analysis of which contact types, agents, or time periods are driving SLA breaches and failure demand.
The Action
Designed 6 progressive SQL queries — from monthly summaries to advanced window functions — to surface repeat contact drivers, agent performance gaps, hourly staffing risks, and segment-level failure demand patterns.
The Result
3 contact reason codes identified as driving 67% of all repeat contacts. Agent tenure correlation surfaced onboarding gap. Segment matrix enabled targeted fixes rather than blanket process changes.

Designed and documented independently
The Problem
No documented process existed for the MIBCO regulatory exemption workflow spanning 5 stakeholder functions — resulting in ambiguity, delays, and no audit trail across a high-compliance regulatory environment.
The Action
Designed an end-to-end swim lane process flow from zero — mapping 12 structured steps across Customer Service, Fund Governance, FSCA, and CSUF Board — creating a traceable, auditable governance workflow.
The Result
Process adopted as the standard operating reference for regulatory exemption handling. Eliminated procedural ambiguity across 5 stakeholder functions in a FSCA-regulated environment.

A Note on Self-Initiated Delivery
What makes this portfolio different
Every artifact in this portfolio was self-initiated — not assigned, not requested, and not the result of a formal project brief. Each one began with a question: "Why doesn't this exist?" The Issues Register, the LPI Dashboard, the process flows, the RCA framework — all were identified as gaps, scoped independently, and delivered without prompting.

This is the distinction between a BA who executes briefs and one who creates the brief. It is also the reason all metrics in this portfolio are real — they come from work that actually happened, not simulated scenarios.
